## 1. Price Distribution
- Entire home/apartment listings have the highest median prices and the widest price range.
- Shared rooms are significantly cheaper and represent a very small portion of total listings.

## 2. Price and Demand Relationship
- Listings with lower prices tend to accumulate more reviews.
- High-priced listings generally receive fewer reviews, indicating price sensitivity in demand.

## 3. Room Type Structure
- Entire home/apartment and private room listings dominate the market.
- Shared rooms are niche and limited in availability.

## 4. Geographic Patterns
- Manhattan and Brooklyn show the highest concentration of listings.
- Premium-priced listings are more clustered in central areas, especially Manhattan.

## 5. Business Interpretation
- Competitive pricing can increase visibility and demand.
- Higher prices may generate higher revenue per booking but reduce booking frequency.
